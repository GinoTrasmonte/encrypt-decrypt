Why is encryption important?

Encryption protects sensitive data, ensures privacy, and maintains data integrity. It's essential for secure communications, financial transactions, and protecting personal information (Source: NSA Cybersecurity Guidelines).

Can encryption be misused?

Yes, encryption can be used maliciously by criminals to hide illegal activities. Terrorist groups have been known to use encrypted communications to avoid surveillance (Source: FBI Public Report 2023).

Ethical Concerns

The debate between privacy rights and law enforcement access ("crypto wars") continues. Apple vs FBI case (2016) highlighted tensions between corporate responsibility and national security (Source: Electronic Frontier Foundation).
